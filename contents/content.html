Cries of the Wilted Flower

John Augustine Sinang

09-15-2021

I told death that I would wait for the end of the line
A sigh of relief, is what I thought
Then strikes the trauma, dining on this flesh of mine
A heavy burden, is what it's brought.

I told death that I would wait for the end of the line
But scars struck deep; my feet are going back
My eyes flood red - blood, they taste like wine
Or I may just be drunk from the love that I lack

Crawling fast and steady, they go to the blackened eyes
Where the darkest nightmare dwells, they just repeat
So with the leftovers I have, I cover them with lies
Just so I can tread life even when wilting leaves consume my feet
